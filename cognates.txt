Indices

The first index contains the Polish-English cognates – analysed in the present
book and/or presented in broader context (Slavic, Germanic and Indo-European).
Not only the exact cognates are included, but also partial cognates. The minimum
requirement is that they should exhibit a common root, possibly alternant forms
of a common root. The second index differs from the first in that the cognates included are not analysed in the text in greater detail, but just mentioned as further
or parallel examples. The third index contains Polish-English comparanda related
by borrowing, both direct and indirect and also independent loanwords from
a third source, via various intermediaries. The fourth index includes independent
derivatives in both languages with a common root. As discussed in Chapter 10, in
some cases, the pairs which look like independent derivatives can be simultaneously exact cognates. Such comparanda are joined with the =^ symbol. The last
index includes reconstructed forms of Proto-Indo-European, i.e. a common ancestor for Polish-English cognates.
